# AI Usage Policy
**Policy ID:** AI-GOV-001
**Version:** 1.0
**Effective Date:** 2026-01-01
**Policy Owner:** AI Governance Committee

## 1. Purpose
This policy defines acceptable and restricted use of AI tools, including public AI tools, approved internal AI tools, customer data handling, confidential data rules, human review, and AI use case approval.

## 2. Approved AI Tools
Employees may use only company-approved AI tools for business work.

Public AI tools may be used only for low-risk tasks that do not involve confidential, personal, customer, financial, legal, security, or regulated data.

## 3. Customer Data Restriction
Employees must not upload customer data, personal data, confidential company data, source code, production logs, contracts, financial data, credentials, or security findings to public AI tools.

Customer data may be used with an approved internal AI tool only if:
- the use case is approved by the AI Governance Committee
- data minimization is followed
- access controls are applied
- the output is reviewed by a human
- retention and privacy requirements are met

## 4. Confidential Information
Confidential information must not be entered into public AI systems.

Examples of confidential information include:
- customer names and contact details
- invoices and payment details
- employee records
- contracts
- business strategy documents
- source code
- security vulnerabilities
- internal financial reports

## 5. AI Use Case Approval
Any AI use case involving customer data, employee data, financial data, automated decision-making, legal content, security analysis, or external publication must be reviewed before production use.

Required approvals may include:
- business owner approval
- data privacy review
- information security review
- AI governance review
- legal review where applicable

## 6. Human Review
AI-generated outputs must be reviewed by a human before being used for:
- customer communication
- legal or compliance content
- financial analysis
- HR decisions
- security recommendations
- public communication

## 7. Prohibited Uses
Employees must not use AI tools to:
- bypass security controls
- generate malicious code
- make final HR, legal, or financial decisions without human review
- process customer data in public tools
- create misleading, discriminatory, or harmful content

## 8. Unclear Scenarios
If a scenario is not clearly covered by this policy, employees must contact the AI Governance Committee or Information Security team before proceeding.
